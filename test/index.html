What is it?

Electron Beam Tomography, know within the medical community
as EBT, is a type of CT (computed tomography) scan that is on
the cutting edge of medical imaging technology and has very
useful applications in the fields of medicine and biology.



Electron Beam Tomography Wikipedia Page

Who invented it?

EBT was invented by Dr.Douglas Boyd at Imatron, starting in
1977.


Where was it first implemented?

The first clinical installation took place at University of
California at San Francisco circa 1984.


How has it changed since inception?

EBT has been improved in spatial resolution, temporal
resolution, and software applicability, and has been sold to
physicians globally.

Why is it useful?

EBT has a wide variety of applications within the medical
imaging community.

Applications Include......

  • Original Anatomy
  • Wall Motion
  • Perfusion
  • Measurement of Coronary Calcium
  • CT Coronary Angiography
  • Lung Studies
  • Standard CT Radiological Examinations
